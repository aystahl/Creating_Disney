Creating Disney

Walt Disney with several of his most popular characters.

Here is a glimpse of Walt Disney's life:

  • 1901 - Born Walter Elias Disney on December 5 in Chicago, Illinois, U.S.
  • 1911 - Moved to Kansas City, Missouri with his family. There he met Walter Pfeiffer who introducted him to the world of vaudeville and motion pictures. Around this time Disney attended the Kansas City Art Institute and also took a correspondence course in cartooning.
  • 1917 - Disney became the cartoonist of the McKinley High School newspaper, drawing patriotic pictures about World War I.
  • 1918 - Joined the Red Cross as an ambulance driver. During this time he had some of his work published in the army newspaper Stars and Stripes.
  • 1919 - Returned to Kansas City and started an apprenticeship at Pesmen-Rubin Commercial Art Studio. He met fellow artist Ub Iwerks.
  • 1920 - Started the short-lived Iwerks-Disney Commercial Artists. After failing to attract business both artists began working at Kansas City Film Ad Company.
  • 1921 - Established Laugh-O-Gram Studio. Disney started production of Alice's Wonderland a live action comedy combined with animation. This venture was short-lived and went into bankruptcy in 1923.
  • 1923 - Disney moved to Holloywood and, along with his brother Roy, forms Disney Brothers Studio. Which later becomes what we all know today as The Walt Disney Company.
  • 1925 - Hires ink artist, Lillian Bounds. Walt and Lillian marry later that year.
  • 1927 - Oswald the Lucky Rabbit was created.
  • 1928 - Disney realised Universal owned the intellectual property rights to Oswald. Among losing Oswald, Disney also loses most of his animation staff.
  • 1928 - Mickey Mouse was born. Mickey made his first public appearance in Steamboat Willie after failing to find a distributor for the first two screenings Plane Crazy and The Gallopin' Gaucho.
  • 1932 - Disney gained sole right to use Technicolor until 1935.
  • 1932 - Won the Academy Award for best Short Subject (Cartoon).
  • 1933 - Won a second Academy Award for the best Short Subject (Cartoon).
  • 1934 - Began a four-year production of his first feature-length cartoon, Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs.
  • 1938 - Began working on Pinocchio and Fantasia.
  • 1941 - Releases Dumbo after a temporary strike interrupts production.
  • 1942 - Bambi hits the box office.
  • 1950 - Produces Cinderella the studio's first animated feature in eight years.
  • 1951 - Completed Alice in Wonderland.
  • 1953 - Releases Peter Pan.
  • 1954 - Construction starts on Disneyland.
  • 1955 - Disneyland opens to the public.
  • 1964 - Disney produced Mary Poppins.
  • 1965 - Announced plans to develop a second theme park called "Disney World" which is now called Walt Disney World.
  • 1966 - Died December 15 at age 65 in Burbank, California, U.S.
